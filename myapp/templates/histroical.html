Historical Perspectives on Reading Instruction

The "Reading Wars"

Phonics vs. Whole Language: A debate in the late 20th century about whether phonics (systematic instruction of letter-sound relationships) or whole language (emphasizing meaning and literature) was more effective.

Early Methods

Whole Word Approach: Emphasized recognizing whole words by sight. Popular in the early 20th century.

Phonics Approach: Focused on the relationship between letters and sounds (phonemes). Dominated reading instruction in the mid-20th century.
